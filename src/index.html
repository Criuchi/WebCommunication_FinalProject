HISTORY

Knobby’s Kids Hockey was founded in 2006 by friends, Knobby Knudsen, Jerry Slavik and Frank Spry. Our founders recognized the need for sports for children who may not otherwise be able to join a league. The initial season saw 50 kids hit the ice over two hours on Saturday mornings. Over the years the program expanded to three hours of ice time and additional special events were added to support kids and their families. In 2023 registration hit a new high of 150 kids registered and 50 additional kids on a waiting list.

FOUNDERS

KNOBBY KNUDSEN

Knobby Knudsen had the original inspiration to create our program. He wanted a program that allowed children to learn and play hockey without having the financial burden of registering in a league and buying equipment. In addition, the program would also let kids play without the competitive stress of traditional minor hockey. At the time it seemed like a crazy idea but that didn’t deter Knobby. He teamed up with Jerry Slavik and Frank Spry and built the program from nothing.

JERRY SLAVIK

Jerry Slavik has been the leader of our team, the captain of captains and the best coach one could ever have. We have all benefited from the knowledge, generosity, and kindness that he has shown. He has been a mentor to us and taught us how to be better persons. We have learned from him that the impossible is possible when you set your mind to it. He turned good ideas into great actions and has given, to hundreds of kids, the opportunity to play the great game of hockey.

FRANK SPRY

Frank Spry was one of the originals that helped make Knobby's Kids the program that it has become. Frank was always there early to greet all the kids with his giant booming voice. He kept all the volunteers inline and always added a little humour to our mornings. He was special to all our kids, especially the littlest ones who couldn't wait to run up and say hi to Mr. Frank. Truly a fine gentleman who will be greatly missed in our community.
